About Me

A Brief Introduction

I am an AI researcher passionate about Natural Language Processing, with a deep focus on bias and fairness, low-resource languages, and computational social science. My academic journey has been driven by a commitment to understanding and mitigating inequalities in language technologies across diverse linguistic communities. I believe that ethical and inclusive AI is not just a technical challenge, but a social responsibility. Through my research, I aim to build language models that are more equitable, transparent, and accessible for underrepresented languages and speakers.

Research Interests

What I Work On

Natural Language Processing

Developing methods for understanding, generating, and reasoning with human language across diverse contexts and languages.

Bias and Fairness

Identifying, measuring, and mitigating systematic biases in language models to ensure equitable outcomes for all users.

Low-resource Languages

Bridging the digital divide by building NLP tools and resources for underrepresented and low-resource linguistic communities.

Computational Social Science

Leveraging large-scale text data to study social phenomena, public opinion, and the societal impact of language technologies.
